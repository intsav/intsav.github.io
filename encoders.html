Background:

Retinal degeneration is a leading cause of blindness worldwide. There is currently no cure for retinal degeneration, and the only treatment options available are limited.

- Tissue engineering is a promising approach for the regeneration of the retina. Tissue engineering scaffolds can provide the structural and biochemical cues that are necessary for retinal cells to grow and differentiate.

- 3D bioprinting is a rapidly developing technology that can be used to create tissue engineering scaffolds with complex structures. However, the design of 3D bioprinted tissue engineering scaffolds is still a challenging task.

- AI-driven methods have the potential to revolutionize the design of 3D bioprinted tissue engineering scaffolds. These methods can be used to automate the design process and to create scaffolds that are more efficient, effective, and affordable.

Methods:

The proposed project will use a combination of experimental and computational methods. The experimental work will involve the fabrication of a variety of tissue engineering scaffolds using different materials, printing parameters, and fabrication techniques. The computational work will involve the development of AI algorithms for the prediction of scaffold properties and the optimization of scaffold design. The specific methods that will be used include:

- 3D bioprinting to fabricate tissue engineering scaffolds.

- Image analysis to characterize the structure of the tissue.

- Mechanical testing to measure the mechanical properties of scaffolds.

- Machine learning to predict the properties of scaffolds and optimize their design.

Expected outcomes:

The expected outcomes of the project are to develop AI-driven methods that can be used to automate the design of tissue engineering scaffolds. These methods will be able to predict the properties of scaffolds and optimize their design for specific applications. The methods will be made available to the research community and will be used to design new and improved tissue engineering scaffolds.

Significance:

The development of AI-driven methods for the design of tissue engineering scaffolds has the potential to revolutionize the field of tissue engineering. These methods will make it possible to design scaffolds that are more efficient, effective, and affordable. The methods will also make it possible to design scaffolds that are tailored to specific applications, such as the repair of damaged retinas or the regeneration of entire retinas.
