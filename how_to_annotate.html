How to Annotate

Project Description and Goals


Each of the projects listed on the biobank homepage aims to create a standardized, open-access dataset of A4C images with varying degrees of foreshortening, septal wall orientation/alignment, visibility of endocardial segments and valve visibility along with expert annotations for each. The dataset thus created will be a high-quality diverse dataset that can be used to train and validate AI algorithms to perform various image processing tasks, Improve Image Acquisition, accuracy and reliability of A4C echocardiographic interpretations for better patient care.



Your Role

  • Review A4C Echocardiographic Videos/Images: You will be presented with a series of A4C echocardiographic videos/images.
  • Assessment and Ranking: Each project has a demonstration video explaining how to access the project on unityimaging.net along with the guidelines. Using the guidelines provided and your expertise, you will assess each video/image.
  • Provide Feedback (Optional): Share your valuable insights and suggestions for improving the annotation process or the assessment criteria.

Who Should Participate?

  • Echocardiographers: Your expertise in image interpretation is crucial for accurate assessment.
  • Cardiologists: Your clinical knowledge can help understand the impact of foreshortening, endocardial borders visibility, etc on diagnosis.
  • Sonographers: Your experience in image acquisition can provide valuable insights into optimizing image quality.
