Image Quality Assessment Framework - A4C Septal Wall Orientation

Project Description


The septal wall orientation is a critical factor in the image quality assessment of echocardiographic images. The septal wall refers to the septum, which is the wall separating the left and right sides of the heart. Proper orientation and visualization of the septal wall are essential for accurate diagnosis and evaluation of cardiac function. This project aims to create a standardised, open-access dataset of A4C images with varying degrees of septal wall orientation, along with expert annotations.



Project Goals

  • Develop a Reference Dataset:
    • Objective: Create a comprehensive dataset of A4C echocardiographic images focusing on various views that highlight the septal wall, with diverse orientations and quality levels.
    • Action: Collect and annotate images by experienced echocardiographers to ensure high-quality and detailed labeling of septal wall orientation and related metrics.
  • Standardize Septal Wall Orientation Assessment:
    • Objective: Establish clear and objective criteria for assessing the quality and orientation of the septal wall in echocardiographic images.
    • Action: Develop guidelines and benchmarks to evaluate proper septal wall visualization, ensuring consistency and reproducibility across different imaging sessions and machines.
  • Empower AI Development:
    • Objective: Provide a high-quality, annotated dataset to train and validate AI algorithms for automatic detection, assessment, and correction of septal wall orientation in echocardiographic images.
    • Action: Collaborate with data scientists and AI researchers to refine algorithms that can identify suboptimal orientations and suggest corrective actions.
  • Improve Image Acquisition:
    • Objective: Develop comprehensive training materials and guidelines to help sonographers optimize image acquisition, focusing on proper septal wall orientation.
    • Action: Create instructional videos, manuals, and workshops to educate sonographers on best practices for achieving optimal septal wall visualization and minimizing orientation errors.
  • Enhance Diagnostic Accuracy:
    • Objective: Improve the accuracy and reliability of echocardiographic interpretations by ensuring optimal septal wall orientation in acquired images.
    • Action: Implement standardized assessment criteria and AI tools in clinical practice to support echocardiographers in making more accurate diagnoses and treatment decisions.
  • Validate and Benchmark Algorithms:
    • Objective: Regularly validate and benchmark AI algorithms against the reference dataset to ensure continuous improvement and adaptation to new imaging technologies.
    • Action: Establish a cyclical review process involving clinical experts and data scientists to update and refine algorithms based on real-world feedback and advancements.
  • Promote Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration:
    • Objective: Foster collaboration between echocardiographers, sonographers, AI developers, and healthcare providers to enhance the overall quality of echocardiographic imaging.
    • Action: Organize symposiums, workshops, and collaborative research projects to share knowledge, tools, and best practices across disciplines.

Your Role

  • Review A4C Echocardiographic Images: You will be presented with a series of A4C echocardiographic images
  • Assess Septal wall Orientation: Using the provided guidelines and your expertise, you will assess the degree of Septal wall Orientation: in each image.
  • Rank Images: You will then rank the images based on the orientation of the septal wall, from those oriented anticlockwise to those oriented clockwise.
  • Provide Feedback (Optional): Share your valuable insights and suggestions for improving the annotation process or the assessment criteria.

How to Participate

  1. Create an Account: Register on the UnityImaging.net labelling platform.
  2. Access the Project: Find and join the "A4C Septal Wall Orientation Assessment Project."
  3. Review Guidelines: Familiarize yourself with the detailed annotation instructions and criteria for assessing Orientation.
  4. Start Annotating: Begin reviewing and ranking A4C echocardiographic images based on the direction of Orientation.
  5. Provide Feedback (Optional): Share your insights to help us refine the process and improve the dataset.

Ranking Criteria

  • Left Ventricle Visualization: Assess how well the entire left ventricle, from apex to base, is visible.
  • Papillary Muscle Appearance: Consider how distinct and well-defined the papillary muscles appear.
  • Overall Image Quality: Rate the overall clarity and diagnostic quality of the image, considering factors like contrast and resolution.

Who Should Participate?

  • Echocardiographers: Your expertise in image interpretation is crucial for accurate assessment.
  • Cardiologists: Your clinical knowledge can help understand the impact of proper septal wall orientation on diagnosis.
  • Sonographers: Your experience in image acquisition can provide valuable insights into optimizing image quality.
