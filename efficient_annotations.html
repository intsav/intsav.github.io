Efficient Approaches for Creating a Biobank of Expert Annotations for Deep Learning Developments in Echocardiography

Project Introduction


Deep learning models require large annotated datasets with accurate annotations for the training. It is rare to have perfectly annotated datasets in medical images since it is expensive and time-consuming. Thus, imperfect datasets negatively affect the accuracy of medical image analysis, classification, and segmentation; an essential image processing step for most deep learning models in computer vision. This research aims to discover state-of-the-art techniques for handling the challenges of dataset annotations (scarce annotations and noisy annotations) in the domain of medical images, particularly echocardiographic images.

Dataset

Real-world patient datasets will be used. It will be provided by the project collaborators and clinical partners, the School of Medicine, Imperial College, London. They will provide access to their patient datasets and studies. In addition, the clinical staff will be available to provide support and advice for cardiac images annotations.
The dataset that will be used for this project is the echocardiogram for the Left Ventricle (LV) of the heart. It includes 3065 images that will be used for training, testing, and validation. The labels are obtained as coordinates of the LV from JSON files, then the data preprocessing is performed by creating the masks and cleaning the dataset.


Network Architecture



For medical image analysis, the most appropriate architecture that has been used is U-Net, a fully convolutional neural network. It is fast, outperforming the prior best medical imaging segmentation methods (Ronneberger, Fischer and Brox, 2015).

Figure 1 shows the U-Net architecture that has a U-shape for encoder and decoder processes. It is the most used architecture in cardiovascular image analysis for segmentation tasks.

In this project U-Net architecture with batch normalization will be used as the base model for LV image segmentation.
