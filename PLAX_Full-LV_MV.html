Image Quality Assessment Framework - PLAX Full-LV - MV

Project Description


Visualizing the entire left ventricle (LV) along with the mitral valve (MV) in PLAX images is vital for comprehensive cardiac evaluation. This project aims to create a standardized, open-access dataset of PLAX images focusing on full LV and MV views, annotated by experts.



Project Goals

  • Develop a Reference Dataset: Compile a comprehensive dataset of PLAX images highlighting full LV and MV views, annotated by experienced echocardiographers.
  • Standardize Full LV Assessment: Establish objective criteria for evaluating the quality of full LV and MV views in PLAX images.
  • Empower AI Development: Provide a high-quality dataset to train and validate AI algorithms for detecting and enhancing full LV views.
  • Improve Image Acquisition: Develop training materials and guidelines to help sonographers optimize image acquisition for clear full LV and MV views.
  • Enhance Diagnostic Accuracy: Improve the reliability of PLAX echocardiographic interpretations for better patient care.

Your Role

  • Review PLAX Echocardiographic Images: You will be presented with a series of PLAX echocardiographic images.
  • Assess Full LV Views: Use the provided guidelines and your expertise to assess the quality of full LV and MV views in each image.
  • Rank Images: Rank the images based on the clarity and completeness of the full LV and MV views.
  • Provide Feedback (Optional): Share your insights and suggestions for improving the annotation process or the assessment criteria.

How to Participate

  1. Create an Account: Register on the UnityImaging.net labelling platform.
  2. Access the Project: Join the "PLAX Full-LV MV Assessment Project."
  3. Review Guidelines: Familiarize yourself with the detailed annotation instructions and criteria for assessing full LV and MV views.
  4. Start Annotating: Begin reviewing and ranking PLAX images based on the quality of full LV and MV views.
  5. Provide Feedback (Optional): Share your insights to help refine the process and improve the dataset.

Ranking Criteria

  • Ventricle Visualization: Assess how well the entire left ventricle and mitral valve are visualized.
  • Overall Image Quality: Rate the overall clarity and diagnostic quality of the image, considering factors like contrast and resolution.

Who Should Participate?

  • Echocardiographers: Your expertise in image interpretation is crucial for accurate assessment.
  • Cardiologists: Your clinical knowledge can help understand the impact of full LV views on diagnosis.
  • Sonographers: Your experience in image acquisition can provide valuable insights into optimizing image quality.
