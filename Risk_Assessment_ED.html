AI for Risk Assessment in Emergency Department Presentations

University of West London – School of Computing and Engineering

In Collaboration with the National Heart and Lung Institute

Supervisory Team

Professor Massoud Zolgharni
Professor Darrel Francis

Background:

The Emergency Department (ED) is a critical setting for rapid clinical decision-making across a wide range of patient presentations. Timely and accurate risk stratification helps ensure appropriate resource use and better patient outcomes. However, clinical assessments are often subjective, and variability in decision-making can impact quality of care. AI offers the potential to standardize and enhance these decisions using data-driven tools.

Research Questions:

- Can AI models effectively stratify risk across diverse ED presentations?

- What clinical patterns and features are most predictive of adverse outcomes in the ED?

- How can AI-based support tools be integrated into real-time clinical workflows?

Aim and Objectives:

This PhD project aims to develop and validate AI-powered systems to enhance clinical risk assessment across Emergency Department scenarios. Specific objectives include:

- Acquire and harmonize large-scale datasets such as MIMIC-IV-ED, MC-MED, and HCUP NEDS.

- Develop supervised models to predict outcomes such as admission, imaging needs, or ICU transfer.

- Evaluate models using AUROC, calibration, and clinical utility analysis.

- Compare AI models with standard clinical scores and physician judgment.

Methodology:

- Retrospective analysis of publicly available emergency datasets.

- Use of machine learning methods including deep learning and gradient boosting.

- External validation and generalizability testing across hospital settings.

- Decision curve analysis to assess real-world clinical utility.

Clinical Partners:

Collaboration with Imperial College London provides clinical relevance, expert feedback, and access to validation cohorts. This partnership ensures model outputs are aligned with real-world ED practices and workflows.
